Lab 2 — Post-Quantum Retail Encryption

Vague Hypothesis

A post-quantum key-establishment scheme combined with a high-strength symmetric cipher will provide security and performance suitable for protecting retail data against quantum-capable adversaries, when compared with a legacy key-exchange design.

Background (Conceptual)

Modern public-key systems used in retail environments are vulnerable in principle to sufficiently powerful quantum computers. Lattice-based key-establishment mechanisms and other post-quantum approaches aim to offer long-term confidentiality while still enabling practical deployment. Hybrid designs pair such mechanisms with established symmetric ciphers to secure bulk data once a shared secret is derived.

Design Overview (Quasi-Comparative)

  • Independent variable: Key-establishment architecture (legacy public-key based versus hybrid post-quantum plus symmetric).
  • Dependent variables: Qualitative security properties, message sizes, and approximate latency/throughput behavior for typical retail operations.
  • Design: Both architectures are analyzed under the same threat model and workload assumptions; detailed protocol parameters remain private.

Student-Facing Tasks (High Level)

  1. Summarize why traditional public-key systems are at risk in a post-quantum setting.
  2. Describe, in general terms, how a lattice-based or similar post-quantum mechanism can be combined with a symmetric cipher in a retail architecture.
  3. Identify criteria (security, performance, deployability) that a retailer should consider when comparing a legacy design with a hybrid post-quantum design.
  4. Outline an evaluation plan that compares both architectures without revealing specific implementation details.

Deliverables

  • An architecture-focused report explaining the two designs, your evaluation criteria, and your reasoned judgment about the hypothesis.
  • Implementation specifics, configuration values, and code remain in private lab materials and are not described in this HTML.
