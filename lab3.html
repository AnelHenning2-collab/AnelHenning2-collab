Lab 3 — Quantum Error Correction and Modeling

Vague Hypothesis

Encoding information with a moderate-distance quantum error-correcting code and modeling error behavior will reduce effective error rates compared with using unencoded qubits subject to similar physical noise.

Background (Conceptual)

Quantum error-correcting codes trade additional physical resources for improved reliability of logical information. Simple codes show, in theory and in simulation, that under certain noise regimes the probability of a logical error can be significantly lower than the probability of an error on any single physical carrier. Error modeling and prediction can further inform how such codes are deployed in larger systems.

Design Overview (Simulation-Based)

  • Independent variable: Protection scheme and modeling strategy (unencoded versus encoded, with or without an error model).
  • Dependent variables: Empirical logical error rates, estimated improvement factors, and quality of any model used to predict failures.
  • Design: Simulated experiments apply comparable noise to different protection schemes and aggregate outcomes; specific circuits, noise parameters, and models are maintained privately.

Student-Facing Tasks (High Level)

  1. Explain the conceptual difference between a physical qubit and a logical qubit protected by an error-correcting code.
  2. Describe how you would, in principle, estimate logical error rates under a given noise process.
  3. Propose a way to compare protected and unprotected information so that any observed reliability improvement can be quantified.
  4. Outline how a simple error model or predictor could help anticipate when protection might fail, without specifying concrete algorithms or parameters.

Deliverables

  • A brief report describing the conceptual simulation design, key outcome measures, and your interpretation of whether the hypothesis is supported under the assumed conditions.
  • Any detailed simulation code, circuit constructions, and parameter choices are kept in private lab documentation and not exposed here.
