Lab 1 — Quantum-Enhanced Routing

Vague Hypothesis

A quantum-enhanced optimization method will achieve slightly lower routing costs than a conventional heuristic on small retail-style routing instances.

Background (Conceptual)

Routing problems in logistics are often solved with approximate heuristics rather than exact algorithms due to their computational complexity. Recent work on quantum-inspired and quantum-approximate optimization approaches suggests that they may offer modest improvements on certain combinatorial problems when applied carefully to well-structured instances.

Design Overview (Quasi-Experimental)

  • Independent variable: Optimization strategy (conventional heuristic versus quantum-enhanced method).
  • Dependent variables: Route cost and solution quality relative to a reference; approximate computation effort.
  • Design: Each routing instance is solved under both strategies and the paired results are compared, without exposing specific algorithm parameters or hardware details.

Student-Facing Tasks (High Level)

  1. Explain in your own words how a routing problem can be formulated as an optimization task for retail logistics.
  2. Describe a reasonable classical heuristic for such a problem and how you would evaluate its performance.
  3. Describe, at a conceptual level, how a quantum-enhanced optimization method could explore the same search space differently.
  4. Propose metrics you would use to compare both strategies on the same set of routing scenarios.

Deliverables

  • A short report describing the problem, both strategies at a high level, the comparison design, and your interpretation of potential outcomes.
  • Any implementation details or code you create are kept in a private repository and are not included in the public-facing description.
